# Chaya Kong Advisory - project instructions

Global rules in ~/.config/agents/AGENTS.md apply. This adds project specifics.

## What this site is
Trilingual (EN / ZH-Hans / HE) site for a China-Israel commercial advisory practice.
Buyer: Chinese companies establishing Israeli operations, or Israeli companies
working with China.

## Positioning (single, settled - do not relitigate or widen)
- ONE position: China-Israel supply chain and procurement. She runs the
  commercial interface between Chinese and Israeli companies: supplier and
  buyer negotiations in Mandarin, pricing, payment terms, MOQs, lead times,
  contract terms, product quality management, and the data work that supports
  procurement decisions.
- Two faces of the same transaction, one pain: the other side of your
  supply chain is a black box.
  - For Chinese manufacturers: entering and serving the Israeli market -
    local buyers, distribution, terms, and when traction justifies it,
    establishing an Israeli subsidiary (entity setup, banking and regulatory
    navigation, introductions to licensed local professionals).
  - For Israeli companies: sourcing from and negotiating with China -
    supplier vetting, Mandarin negotiation, terms renegotiation, quality
    management, landed-cost and procurement analytics.
- Subsidiary establishment is framed as the later stage of a Chinese
  manufacturer's Israel journey, never as a standalone co-equal service.
- Out of scope for this site: investment scouting, fund advisory, deal
  sourcing. No Torah/TorahPod content - range dilutes. Do not add pages,
  sections, or copy for investor audiences.
- The moat, stated as fact not hype: native Mandarin and Cantonese, Hong Kong
  common-law legal training, fluent Hebrew, and hands-on supply chain and
  data operations inside Israeli companies. The combination is the product;
  no page sells the skills separately. Never structure the site by skill.
- Proof register: concrete and qualitative. Supplier portfolio terms
  renegotiations, Mandarin manufacturer negotiations, inventory and
  procurement analytics, cross-border contract fluency from years in
  corporate law. No fabricated numbers, no client names; anonymize by
  sector and scale only. Only claims she can defend in a meeting.
- NOT a law firm. Never "legal advice", "representation", "counsel", or
  anything implying an Israeli practising licence. She introduces clients
  to licensed counsel; she does not act as counsel.
- Never lead with or self-describe as "compliance". Regulatory navigation
  is a service to clients, not her identity.
- Do not claim deep transfer-pricing or tax expertise. Claim level:
  "structuring and operations consultation".

## Voice
- Direct, concise, zero small talk. Front-load context in one line, then
  the point. Normal paragraphs, medium sentences - never one-sentence-per-line
  cadence, never emoji in professional English copy.
- Confident plain statements ("Built X", "Negotiated Y") - never hedged,
  never hyped. No "passionate", "thrilled", "world-class", "seamless",
  "unlock", or exclamation marks.
- Precision is the brand: a lawyer-turned-analyst sounds specific, names the
  mechanism, cites the concrete thing. One concrete detail beats three
  abstractions.
- English: senior and direct (default). Chinese (Simplified): warm,
  credibility-first, relationship-framed; written natively, never translated
  English sentence structure; never 咨询 for the service. Hebrew:
  professional and direct, no marketing register.
- CTA style: small specific ask (intro call), never urgency theatrics.
  Fees never appear on the site.
